# Cold Brew — Complete Guide

## Science of Cold Extraction

Cold brew uses cold water (4–20°C) over 12–24 hours. Chemically distinct from hot-brewed coffee cooled down:

| Property | Cold Brew | Iced Coffee (hot-brewed + chilled) |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| Acids extracted | Fewer (low solubility at cold temp) | Full spectrum |
| Bitter compounds | Fewer | Standard |
| Volatile aromatics | Fewer | More fragrant |
| Body | High (long contact time) | Variable |
| Perceived acidity | Very low | Higher |
| Perceived sweetness | Higher | Lower |
| Shelf life | 1–2 weeks refrigerated | Best same day |

> 🔬 *Cold brew is not simply "chilled coffee." The extraction at low temperature is chemically selective — it favors large, non-volatile compounds and avoids much of the acidic, volatile fraction.*

## Recipes

### Recipe 1 — Concentrate (1:8)

| Parameter | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Dose | 100g coarse ground |
| Water | 800g cold water |
| Temperature | 4°C (refrigerator) |
| Steep | 18–24 hours |
| Yield | ~700–750mL concentrate |

**Steps:**
1. Combine coffee and cold water in a large mason jar or pitcher
2. Stir to wet all grounds
3. Cover and refrigerate 18–24 hours
4. Filter: pour through fine mesh strainer, then through paper coffee filter
5. Store concentrate sealed in fridge up to 14 days
6. **Serve:** Dilute 1:1 to 1:3 with water, milk, or ice

---

### Recipe 2 — Ready-to-Drink (1:15)

| Parameter | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Dose | 75g coarse ground |
| Water | 1,125g cold water |
| Temperature | 4°C |
| Steep | 12–18 hours |

Serve straight over ice — no dilution needed.

---

### Recipe 3 — Room Temperature Toddy Style

- Same ratio as concentrate (1:8)
- Steep at 18–22°C for 12–16 hours
- Slightly brighter, marginally more acidic than fridge method
- Filter and refrigerate after steeping

| Equipment | Notes |

| Toddy Cold Brew System | Cloth filter built-in; easy filtration |

## Serving Styles

| Style | Recipe |
|-------|--------|
| Classic iced | Concentrate over ice |
| Cold Brew Latte | 1 part concentrate + 2 parts oat/dairy milk |
| Sparkling Cold Brew | RTD cold brew + sparkling water 1:1 |
| **Nitro Cold Brew** | Cold brew + nitrogen gas via draft tap → creamy, no ice |
| Coffee Tonic | Cold brew + premium tonic + orange peel |
| Cold Brew Float | Cold brew + vanilla ice cream |

## Best Coffees for Cold Brew

| Type | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Brazil natural | Chocolate, caramel — beautifully expressed |
| Guatemala medium | Chocolate, nuts, balanced sweetness |
| Colombia washed | Clean, fruity, approachable |
| Sumatra | Earthy depth; unique cold brew character |
| **Avoid:** Very light roasts | Delicate acids/florals don't express at cold temp |
| **Avoid:** Dark over-roasted | Bitter compounds amplified in concentrate |
