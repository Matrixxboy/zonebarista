# Moka Pot — Complete Guide

> **Credits:** Made by **Utsav Lankapati Matrixxboy** ([GitHub](https://github.com/matrixxboy/))

## How It Works

Water in the sealed bottom chamber is heated until steam pressure (~1–1.5 bar) forces it upward through the coffee bed and into the upper collection chamber.

```
                    ┌──────────┐
                    │ COFFEE   │ ← Upper chamber (collection)
                    │ COLLECTS │
                    ├──────────┤
                    │ [BASKET] │ ← Coffee bed (ground coffee, level, untamped)
                    ├──────────┤
                    │  WATER   │ ← Bottom chamber (sealed, heated)
                    └──────────┘
                        🔥  ← Heat source
```

> ⚠️ **Moka Pot ≠ Espresso.** It operates at 1–1.5 bar vs espresso's 9 bar. The result is bold, concentrated coffee, but lacks true espresso crema and extraction character.

## Recipe

| Parameter | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Coffee amount | Fill basket level (do not pack) ~15–20g for 3-cup |
| Grind | Fine-medium (between filter and espresso; like coarse table salt) |
| Water | Fill bottom chamber to just below safety valve |
| Heat | Medium-low |
| Total time | 5–8 minutes |

**Steps:**
1. Fill bottom with **pre-boiled water** (reduces time on heat → less scorching)
2. Insert filter basket; fill with ground coffee — **level only, never tamp**
3. Wipe basket rim; screw top chamber on tight
4. Place on medium-low heat with lid open
5. Watch: coffee begins appearing in top chamber ~4–6 min
6. **Remove immediately** before the final gurgling/sputtering rush
7. Optional: run cold water over bottom chamber to stop extraction
8. Stir brewed coffee in top chamber before pouring (evening out first vs last extraction)

---

## Critical Rules

| Rule | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Pre-boil water | Reduces time on stove heat → prevents scorching ground contact |
| Never tamp | Excess resistance → pressure builds unsafely; over-extraction |
| Medium-low heat | Slow water rise = better extraction; fast = bitter |
| Remove before gurgle | Final rush is steam-forced = bitter, harsh, diluted |
| Never fill above valve | Safety valve is a pressure relief — keep it clear |

## Equipment Considerations

| Material | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Aluminum** (classic Bialetti) | Traditional; can impart slight metallic taste over years; don't use with induction |
| **Stainless steel** | Better for induction; longer lasting; neutral flavor |
| **Induction adapter** | Needed for aluminum pots on induction hobs |

**Sizes:** 1-cup, 3-cup, 6-cup, 9-cup, 12-cup (cup = ~50mL espresso-style serving)

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Bitter, burnt taste | Too fine grind or too high heat | Coarser grind; lower heat |
| Weak, sour | Too coarse or too little coffee | Finer grind; fill basket fully |
| Gurgling loudly | Heat too high → steam rushing | Lower heat |
| No flow / extremely slow | Grind too fine or tamped | Coarser grind; level only |
| Metallic taste | Old aluminum; buildup | Descale or replace pot |
| Leaking from seal | Worn gasket | Replace rubber gasket |
