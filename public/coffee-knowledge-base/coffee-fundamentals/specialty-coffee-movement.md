## What Is Specialty Coffee?

The **Specialty Coffee Association (SCA)** defines specialty coffee as:

> *"Coffee that has been assessed by a certified Q Grader and scored 80 points or above on the SCA 100-point scale."*

This means specialty coffee must meet rigorous standards across:
- **Green bean quality** (zero primary defects, max 5 secondary defects per 350g)
- **Roast quality** (no quakers, uniform development)
- **Cup quality** (cupping score ≥ 80)

---

## The SCA Scoring System

```
90–100  →  Outstanding (world-class, rare)
85–89   →  Excellent
80–84   →  Specialty (minimum threshold)
Below 80 →  Not specialty coffee (commodity/commercial grade)
```

### SCA Cupping Scorecard Attributes
| Attribute | Max Points | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Fragrance/Aroma | 10 | Dry + wet aroma quality |
| Flavor | 10 | Overall flavor impression |
| Aftertaste | 10 | Finish length and quality |
| Acidity | 10 | Brightness and quality (not just intensity) |
| Body | 10 | Tactile mouthfeel, weight |
| Balance | 10 | Harmony of all attributes |
| Uniformity | 10 | Consistency across 5 cups |
| Clean Cup | 10 | Absence of defects |
| Sweetness | 10 | Presence and quality of sweetness |
| Overall | 10 | Holistic judge impression |
| **Total** | **100** | |

## Key Organizations

| Organization | Role |
|-------------|------|
| **SCA** (Specialty Coffee Association) | Global standards, certifications, education |
| **CQI** (Coffee Quality Institute) | Q Grader program, origin development |
| **CoE** (Cup of Excellence) | Competitive auction for top-scoring coffees |
| **WBC** (World Barista Championship) | Barista competition standard |
| **ICO** (International Coffee Organization) | Trade statistics and policy |

## Direct Trade Model

```mermaid
graph LR
    F["👨‍🌾 Farmer/Producer"] -->|Direct relationship| R["🏭 Roaster"]
    R -->|Story + traceability| C["☕ Café"]
    C -->|Consumer education| E["👤 End Customer"]
    E -->|Premium pricing| F
```

**Direct trade** (vs Fair Trade) means:
- Roaster visits farm, sets price independently (often above FT floor)
- Requires trust, ongoing relationship, quality investment
- Higher transparency on both sides
- Enables micro-lot and experimental processing purchases

## Processing Innovation in Specialty

The Third/Fourth Wave has driven processing experimentation:

| Processing | Flavor Impact | Innovation Level |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Washed | Clean, terroir-forward | Traditional |
| Natural | Fruity, complex | Traditional |
| Honey | Balanced | Moderate |
| Anaerobic Natural | Extreme fruit, ferment | High — 2010s+ |
| Lactic Fermentation | Creamy, yogurt-like | High |
| Carbonic Maceration | Wine-like | Very High — borrowed from wine |
| Yeast-inoculated | Controlled, novel | Cutting edge |
