## The Science of Coffee Tasting

### Taste vs Flavor

| Sense | Mechanism | What it detects |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Taste (gustatory)** | Taste buds on tongue | Sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami |
| **Smell (olfactory)** | Olfactory epithelium | ~10,000 odors |
| **Retronasal smell** | Volatile aromatics from mouth → nose | Most of what we call "flavor" |
| **Touch/texture** | Mechanoreceptors | Body, mouthfeel, temperature, carbonation |

> 🔬 **Key insight:** ~80% of what we perceive as "flavor" is actually **smell** (retronasal olfaction). This is why holding your nose while eating greatly diminishes flavor. In cupping, the **slurp** is critical because it drives aromatics retronasally.

## SCA Flavor Wheel — Full Navigation Guide

### How to Use the Wheel

The SCA/WCR Flavor Wheel works from **inside out:**
1. **Inner ring:** Primary category (what general type?)
2. **Middle ring:** Secondary subcategory (what family?)
3. **Outer ring:** Specific descriptor (what exactly?)

## Sensory Training Exercises

### Exercise Set 1: Taste Perception Training

**Purpose:** Calibrate detection thresholds for the 5 basic tastes

#### Sucrose Threshold Series
Prepare 4 solutions in distilled water (1 liter each):

| Solution | Sucrose | Perception |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| A | 0.5g/L | Threshold |
| B | 2.0g/L | Clearly sweet |
| C | 8.0g/L | Moderate sweet |
| D | 20g/L | Strong sweet |

**Protocol:** Taste blind; rank from least to most sweet; identify threshold.

#### Citric Acid Series
| Solution | Citric Acid | Perception |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| A | 0.1g/L | Threshold sour |
| B | 0.5g/L | Mild sour |
| C | 1.5g/L | Clearly sour |
| D | 4.0g/L | Strong sour |

#### Caffeine Bitterness Series
| Solution | Caffeine | Perception |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| A | 0.1g/L | Threshold bitter |
| B | 0.4g/L | Mild bitter |
| C | 1.0g/L | Clearly bitter |

### Exercise Set 2: Organic Acid Identification

**Purpose:** Learn to distinguish specific acids present in coffee (critical for Q Grader exam)

Prepare individual acid solutions at 1g/L in distilled water:

| Acid | Flavor Character | Origin in Coffee |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Citric acid** | Lemon, sharp citrus | Naturally in bean; Ethiopia |
| **Malic acid** | Apple, green fruit, clean | Naturally in bean; moderate roasts |
| **Acetic acid** | Vinegar, wine (low = fermented fruit; high = defect) | Fermentation byproduct |
| **Phosphoric acid** | Crisp, clean brightness, mineral | Kenyan coffees especially |
| **Tartaric acid** | Grape, wine-like | Fermentation |
| **Lactic acid** | Yogurt, creamy, smooth | Lactic fermentation process |
| **Quinic acid** | Dry, astringent, harsh | CGA degradation in roasting |

**Protocol:** Smell and taste each solution; describe in your own words first; then match to the acid name.

### Exercise Set 3: Aroma Training (Le Nez du Café)

The **36-aroma reference kit** covers:

**Enzymatic Group (green bean / light roast aromas):**
- Blackcurrant-like, Garden peas, Cucumber, Unripe, Garden-like

**Sugar-Browning Group (Maillard / medium roast):**
- Roasted almonds, Walnuts, Roasted peanuts, Dark chocolate, Vanilla, Caramel, Buttered toast

**Dry Distillation Group (dark roast / pyrolysis):**
- Spicy, Pipe tobacco, Medicinal, Rubber, Earthy, Chemical

**Exercise protocol:**
1. Smell 3 vials blind → identify by name
2. Match unknown aroma to known reference
3. Do 10 minutes daily for 90 days → significant improvement

---

### Exercise Set 4: Triangle Testing

**Purpose:** Detect differences between coffees; critical for Q Grader triangulation module

**Setup:**
- 3 cups: 2 identical, 1 different
- Cupper must identify the odd one out

**Variables to change:**
- Same coffee, different grind sizes
- Same coffee, different extraction levels (under vs optimal)
- Different origins (easy) → different processing of same origin (hard)
- Same coffee, different roast dates

**Statistical note:** Random chance = 1/3 correct. For statistically significant improvement, track results across 30+ trials.

---

### Exercise Set 5: Origin Identification (Blind Cupping)

**Purpose:** Develop origin recognition; essential for café professionals and Q Graders

**Protocol:**
1. Cup 5 coffees blind (label only A–E)
2. For each, identify:
   - Species (Arabica/Robusta?)
   - Processing (Washed/Natural/Honey?)
   - Continent (Africa/Americas/Asia?)
   - Specific origin (if possible)
3. Reveal; discuss your reasoning

**Start easy:**
- Ethiopia (natural) vs Brazil (natural): very distinct
- Work toward: Colombia vs Honduras (harder)

---

### Exercise Set 6: Defect Detection

**Purpose:** Learn to identify cup defects that disqualify specialty grade

**Setup:** Cup a clean, high-quality coffee as baseline, then:

| Defect to Test | How to Create Sample |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Ferment/sour** | Add 1 full-sour bean per 100g to a batch |
| **Musty** | Use old/improperly stored coffee |
| **Phenolic/medicinal** | Add 1 Rio/phenolic bean |
| **Grassy** | Very light, underdeveloped roast |
| **Baked** | Intentionally baked roast |

**Protocol:** Describe defect flavor; identify cause; discuss preventability.

## Daily Practice Routine (15 min/day)

```
Day 1–30: Foundation
  - 5 min: Aroma kit (3 vials blind identification)
  - 5 min: Taste threshold exercises
  - 5 min: Cup one coffee; write full tasting notes

Day 31–60: Intermediate
  - 5 min: Aroma kit (5 vials, same category comparison)
  - 5 min: Acid identification solutions
  - 5 min: Triangle test with 2 different coffees

Day 61–90: Advanced
  - Full cupping session (20 min) × 3/week
  - Blind origin identification × 2/week
  - Triangle tests × 5/week
  - Defect detection × 1/week
```
