## Equipment: Pitcher Selection

| Pitcher Size | Milk Volume | Best For |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| 300mL (10oz) | 120–180mL | Single small drink (4–6oz cappuccino) |
| 480mL (16oz) | 200–350mL | Standard latte or 2 small drinks |
| 600mL (20oz) | 350–500mL | Large drinks or batch (2 lattes) |
| 1L+ | High volume | Batch steaming (not ideal for latte art) |

**Material:** Stainless steel — conducts heat evenly; allows palm temperature sensing

**Spout shape:**
- **Narrow/pointed spout** → better latte art control (flow is precise)
- **Wide rounded spout** → easier to learn; more forgiving

## Steam Wand Anatomy

```
Steam Wand:
    │ ← Steam tube (carries steam from boiler)
    │
    ◐ ← Pivot joint (angle adjustment)
    │
    │
    ● ← Tip (1–4 holes depending on machine)
```

**Tip types:**
| Tip | Holes | Character |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Single hole | 1 | Gentle; easy control; slow steaming |
| 2-hole | 2 | Balanced; standard specialty |
| 4-hole | 4 | Fast; powerful; commercial standard |
| Panarello/frother | Multiple + air intake | Auto-foam; beginner; poor microfoam quality |

## The Two-Phase Steaming Technique

### Phase 1: STRETCH (Air Incorporation)

**Goal:** Increase milk volume by incorporating air bubbles

- **Wand position:** Tip just below milk surface, slightly off-center
- **Sound:** Hissing, paper-tearing sound (correct); loud screech (too deep); loud splash (too shallow)
- **Duration:** From start until milk reaches ~55°C (warm to touch)
- **Volume increase:** Target 15–50% depending on drink

**Critical:** All air must be incorporated in Phase 1. Do not add air in Phase 2.

---

### Phase 2: ROLL (Texturing)

**Goal:** Integrate foam, break large bubbles, create silky microfoam

- **Wand position:** Deeper into milk, still off-center to maintain vortex
- **Sound:** Gentle rushing/rolling — no hissing
- **Duration:** From ~55°C until 60–65°C
- **Motion:** Rolling vortex breaks large bubbles into microfoam

## Non-Dairy Steaming Adjustments

### Oat Milk (Barista Edition)
| Adjustment | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lower max temp: 55–60°C** | Starches gelatinize above 65°C → gluey texture |
| Less stretch | Protein structure more fragile; over-foaming collapses quickly |
| Slower steam | More delicate than dairy |
| Use fresh (cold) | Warm oat milk from fridge → poor results |

### Soy Milk (Barista Edition)
| Adjustment | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Temperature: 60–65°C | Similar to dairy |
| Acidity sensitivity | Can "break" (curdle) with very acidic espresso; use fresh non-expired |
| Texture | Can become grainy if oversteamed |

### Almond Milk (Barista Edition)
| Adjustment | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Lower stretch | Less protein → less foam stability |
| Temperature: 55–60°C | Sugars degrade faster |
| Result | Thinner foam; some separation |

### Coconut Milk
- Very high fat → heavy, creamy texture
- Poor foam stability
- Strong flavor influence on espresso
- Best for: specialty drinks where coconut is desired, not flat whites

## Post-Steam Technique

After steaming, before pouring:

1. **Wipe steam wand immediately** — pull back towel, purge steam 1–2 seconds
2. **Tap pitcher on counter** — 3–4 firm taps to pop large bubbles
3. **Swirl pitcher vigorously** — 10–15 seconds; integrates foam and liquid
4. **Check surface** — should be glossy, liquid, no visible bubbles
5. **Pour within 30 seconds** — foam degrades quickly

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Large bubbles on surface | Too much air or too shallow wand | Deeper wand; more tapping/swirling |
| Separated foam on top | Not enough rolling or too hot | More rolling phase; lower temp |
| Flat, no foam at all | Wand too deep during stretch | Raise wand to surface level for Phase 1 |
| Watery milk (no body) | Over-steamed, proteins denatured | Start cooler; end at 65°C max |
| Milk too hot to hold cup | Over-steamed | Use thermometer; stop at 65°C |
| Plant milk curdling | Acidity + heat; old milk | Use barista edition; ensure freshness |
