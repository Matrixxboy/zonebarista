# Batch Brewing — Complete Guide

## What Is Batch Brewing?

Batch brewing is automated percolation coffee at scale — a machine sprays or pours hot water evenly over ground coffee in a filter, which drains into a carafe or airpot. In specialty cafés, **SCA-certified brewers** ensure quality standards are met.

---

## SCA Certification Requirements

For a batch brewer to earn SCA certification:

| Requirement | Specification |
|------------|--------------|
| Water temperature at brew head | 90–96°C |
| Total brew time | ≤ 8 minutes |
| Water distribution | Even saturation over grounds |
| Hold temperature (thermal) | 80–85°C |
| Materials | Food-safe, non-reactive throughout |

**SCA-certified models:** Fetco CBS-2131XTS, Bunn AXIOM DBC, Marco Jet 3, BKON Craft Brewer, Newco Gourmet

---

## Recipe (SCA Golden Cup Target)

| Parameter | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Dose | 65g per 1 liter |
| Water | 1,000g (1L) |
| Ratio | 1:15.4 |
| Grind | Medium (between V60 and French Press) |
| Water temperature | 93°C |
| Brew time | 4–6 minutes |

**Scaling examples:**

| Batch Size | Coffee Dose |
|-----------|------------|
| 1 liter | 65g |
| 2 liters | 130g |
| 3 liters | 195g |

## Freshness & Holding Windows

| Container | Peak Freshness | Maximum Hold |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Thermal airpot** | 0–45 minutes | 90 minutes |
| **Glass carafe on warmer** | 0–15 minutes | 20 minutes max |
| **Refrigerated (for iced)** | Same day | 24 hours |

> ❌ **Never:** Reheat brewed coffee. Volatile aromatics are destroyed and harshness increases dramatically.

## High-Volume Café Workflow

```
Peak service batch schedule:
07:00 — Brew batch #1 (3L)
07:00 — Immediately set timer for next brew
07:45 — Brew batch #2; discard any remainder from #1 over 45 min
08:30 — Brew batch #3
[Continue every 45 min throughout service]

End of service:
— Discard all remaining brewed coffee
— Run cleaning cycle per manufacturer
— Rinse carafes/airpots
```

## Grinder Setup for Batch

| Setting vs Espresso | Adjustment |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Much coarser than espresso | Usually 6–10 steps coarser on most grinders |
| Same burrs can be used | Requires significant grind change — some cafés use dedicated batch grinder |
| Dedicated batch grinder | Recommended: EK43, Mahlkönig Guatemala, Mazzer Robur |

---

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Weak, watery | Ratio too low or grind too coarse | Increase dose or grind finer |
| Bitter | Grind too fine or water too hot | Coarser grind; check temperature |
| Inconsistent batch to batch | Grinder retention, dosing error | Weigh every dose; purge grinder |
| Coffee goes stale fast | Thermal carafe not sealed or volume too large for turnover | Brew smaller batches more frequently |
