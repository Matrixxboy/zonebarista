## Science of French Press

French Press is **full immersion** — grounds steep in water for the entire brew. No paper filter means:
- **Coffee oils pass through** into the cup → heavy body, mouthfeel
- **Fine particles (fines)** remain in cup → some sediment
- **Lower acidity perception** — oils coat the tongue, softening acid brightness
- **Less complex aroma** than filtered methods (some volatile aromatics absorbed by oils)

> 💡 *French Press is forgiving and accessible — ideal for beginners and anyone who wants a rich, full-bodied cup without precision pouring technique.*

## Standard Recipe

| Parameter | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Dose | 30g coffee |
| Water | 500g (mL) |
| Ratio | 1:16.7 |
| Grind | **Coarse** (breadcrumb/sea salt texture) |
| Water Temperature | 93°C |
| Total brew time | 4 minutes (standard) / 9–10 min (Hoffman method) |

---

## Brew Methods

### Method A: Classic (4 Minutes)

1. Preheat French Press with hot water; discard
2. Add 30g coarsely ground coffee
3. Pour all 500g of hot water (93°C)
4. Stir gently 10 times
5. Place lid (plunger up); wait 4 minutes
6. Press plunger slowly and steadily
7. Pour immediately — don't let it sit (continues extracting)

**Result:** Full body, some sediment, classic French Press character

---

### Method B: James Hoffman Long Steep (Recommended)

A cleaner variation that reduces fines in the cup:

1. Add 30g coarsely ground coffee
2. Pour all 500g water; don't stir
3. Wait 4 minutes
4. **Break crust** with a spoon (3 gentle strokes)
5. Skim off floating foam and floating grounds with 2 spoons
6. Wait 5 more minutes (total ~9 minutes)
7. **Slowly lower plunger** — only enough to push grounds below liquid surface
8. Pour carefully from top (fines settle to bottom)

**Result:** Cleaner cup with less sediment; fuller body retained

## Grind Size Impact

| Grind | Result |
|-------|--------|
| Too fine | Over-extraction, bitter; plunger hard to push |
| Correct coarse | Balanced, full body |
| Too coarse | Under-extraction, weak, sour |

---

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Sour, thin | Underextracted / too coarse | Finer grind or longer steep |
| Bitter, harsh | Overextracted / too fine | Coarser grind or shorter steep |
| Too much sediment | Grind too fine or over-pressing | Coarser grind; don't fully press |
| Weak cup | Too low dose | Increase dose (try 1:15) |
| Cold cup | Press too cold; not preheated | Preheat press + cups |

---

## Best Coffees for French Press

- **Brazil naturals** — chocolate, caramel, full body → showcased by immersion
- **Sumatra** — earthy, heavy, herbal → enhanced by oil retention
- **Medium roasts** — balanced; not too bright (acidity muted anyway)
- **Avoid:** Delicate washed Ethiopians — their florals and clarity are masked
