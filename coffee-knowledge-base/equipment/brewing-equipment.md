### Hario V60

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Sizes | 01 (1–2 cups), 02 (1–4 cups), 03 (1–6 cups) |
| Materials | Plastic (lightest), ceramic (thermal mass), glass, copper, metal |
| Filter | Bleached or unbleached paper; Hario V60 specific size |
| Flow control | High — spiral ribs allow air escape → barista controls pace entirely |
| Angle | 60° cone |
| Drainage | Single large hole |
| Notes | Industry standard for specialty pour-over; most recipes developed for V60 |
| Price | $10–$60 depending on material |

**Best material choice:**
- **Plastic:** Lightest, no thermal sink, travel-friendly, consistent — often preferred by professionals
- **Ceramic:** Good thermal mass, attractive, keeps slurry warm
- **Copper:** Maximum thermal conductivity, premium, beautiful

## Gooseneck Kettles

Essential for pour-over — precise, controlled water flow:

### Kettle Comparison

| Kettle | Temperature Control | Capacity | Best For |
|--------|-------------------|---------|---------|
| **Fellow Stagg EKG** | Electric; ±1°C PID | 0.9L | Home specialty; precise temperature |
| **Brewista Artisan** | Electric; variable | 0.8–1.0L | Home and light café |
| **Hario Buono** | Stovetop only | 0.8–1.2L | Budget; reliable |
| **Bonavita 1.0L** | Electric; single temp | 1.0L | Entry level |
| **Fellow Stagg EKG Pro** | Bluetooth; app control | 0.9L | Tech-forward users |
| **Barista & Co Core** | Electric; variable | 1.0L | Good value UK option |

**Key features to look for:**
- Temperature control (variable is best — different brews need different temps)
- Gooseneck spout (enables controlled, precise pour rate)
- Minimum 0.6L capacity for any 2-cup pour-over
- Auto-hold temperature (maintains target temp during brew session)

## Scales

Accuracy is non-negotiable in specialty coffee:

### Scale Requirements by Use

| Use | Precision Required | Features Needed |
|-----|-------------------|----------------|
| Espresso | ±0.1g | Fast response time; water-resistant |
| Pour-over | ±0.5g | Timer; flow rate display |
| Cupping | ±0.1g | Any precision scale |
| Green coffee | ±1g | Kitchen scale |
| Roasting | ±1–5g | Commercial bench scale |

### Scale Profiles

| Scale | Precision | Response | Timer | Flow Rate | Price |

| **Acaia Pearl** | 0.1g | Fast (10/s) | ✅ | ✅ | ~$225 |
| **Acaia Lunar** | 0.1g | Very fast (20/s) | ✅ | ✅ | ~$230 (waterproof) |

**Why response time matters for espresso:**
- Espresso shots reach target yield in ~25–35s
- Slow scale response → you overshoot yield before reading updates
- Minimum 5 readings/second for espresso; 10+ preferred

## Filters

### Paper Filters

| Filter Type | Used With | Character |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| Hario V60 (tabbed) | V60 | Standard; consistent |
| Chemex proprietary | Chemex | Thick; removes most oils |
| Kalita Wave | Kalita Wave | Flat-bottom; wave design |
| Melitta #4 | Clever Dripper, many | Widely available |
| Aeropress (round) | AeroPress | Fine; clean |
| Aesir (ultra-thin) | AeroPress | Maximum clarity |

**Bleached vs Unbleached:**
- **Bleached (white):** Pre-rinsed taste is cleaner; no paper taste if rinsed
- **Unbleached (brown):** More "natural"; can have slight paper taste if not pre-rinsed
- **Pre-rinse all filters** with hot water before use → eliminates any paper taste

## Measurement Tools

### Refractometer (TDS Measurement)

| Model | Type | Range | Accuracy | Price |
|-------|------|-------|---------|-------|
| **VST LAB Coffee III** | Digital; coffee-calibrated | 0–25% Brix | ±0.01% | ~$600 |
| **Atago PAL-COFFEE** | Digital; coffee-calibrated | 0–22% Brix | ±0.05% | ~$250 |
| **Atago PAL-1** | Digital; Brix | 0–53% Brix | ±0.1% | ~$150 |
| Optical hand refractometer | Manual; visual | 0–32% Brix | ±0.2% | ~$20 |

> 💡 **VST LAB Coffee III** is the industry gold standard. For home use, Atago PAL-Coffee is excellent value.

**Measurement protocol:**
1. Let sample cool to ~25°C
2. Dilute espresso 1:10 with distilled water (for VST; check model instructions)
3. Apply 2–3 drops to prism
4. Read; apply conversion factor
5. Calculate EY: `EY% = (TDS × Yield) / Dose × 100`
