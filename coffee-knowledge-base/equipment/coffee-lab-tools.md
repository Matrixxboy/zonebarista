## Why Measurement Matters

> *"You cannot improve what you cannot measure."*

Professional coffee measurement enables:
- **Repeatability** — same recipe every time, measurably
- **Troubleshooting** — data-driven diagnosis vs guesswork
- **Communication** — share parameters precisely with team
- **Quality control** — objective benchmarks for green and roasted
- **Research** — systematic experimentation with controlled variables

## 1. Refractometers — TDS & Extraction Yield

### How Coffee Refractometry Works

A refractometer measures **how much light bends** (refractive index) when passing through a liquid. More dissolved solids = higher refractive index = higher Brix reading → converted to TDS %.

$$n = n_0 + \frac{\Delta n}{\Delta C} \times C$$

Where *n* = refractive index, *C* = concentration of dissolved solids.

### Professional Models

| Model | Type | Accuracy | Range | Price |
|-------|------|---------|-------|-------|
| **VST LAB Coffee III** | Digital, Peltier-cooled | ±0.01% Brix | 0–25% | ~$600 |
| **Atago PAL-COFFEE(BX)** | Digital, coffee-calibrated | ±0.05% Brix | 0–22% | ~$250 |
| **Atago PAL-1** | Digital, general Brix | ±0.1% Brix | 0–53% | ~$150 |
| **Atago PAL-COFFEE(TDS)** | Digital, TDS direct | ±0.05% | 0–22% | ~$270 |
| **DiFluid R2 Extract** | Digital, portable | ±0.03% Brix | 0–25% | ~$200 |
| **Optical hand refractometer** | Manual, visual | ±0.2% | 0–32% | ~$20 |

### VST LAB Coffee III — Gold Standard

The **VST LAB Coffee III** is the industry reference:
- **Peltier temperature compensation** — automatically corrects for sample temperature variation
- **Coffee-specific calibration** — accounts for coffee's unique refractive properties vs pure sugar solutions
- **Accuracy:** ±0.01% Brix at controlled temperature
- **Method:** 2–3 drops on prism; reads in seconds

### Measurement Protocol

```
ESPRESSO TDS MEASUREMENT (VST method)

1. Pull espresso shot; collect in small cup
2. Allow to cool to room temperature (25°C) OR dilute 1:10 with distilled water (preferred for high TDS)
3. Zero refractometer with distilled water
4. Place 2–3 drops of sample on prism
5. Close cover; read Brix value
6. Convert: TDS% = Brix × 0.85 (VST coffee conversion factor)
7. Calculate EY: EY% = (TDS% × Yield g) / Dose g × 100

FILTER COFFEE (no dilution needed — TDS 1–2%)
1. Sample at 25°C
2. Zero with distilled water
3. 3–4 drops on prism
4. Read directly (no dilution needed)
5. Calculate EY same formula
```

### Common Errors

| Error | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Reading fluctuates | Sample too hot | Cool to 25°C first |
| Consistently low reading | Prism dirty or scratched | Clean with lens paper |
| Reading higher than expected | Bubbles in sample | Wait for bubbles to settle |
| Zero drift | Temperature change | Re-zero every 15 min |

## 2. Agtron Systems — Roast Color

### What Is Agtron?

**Agtron** spectrophotometers measure **roast colour** by analysing the near-infrared and visible light reflectance of ground or whole coffee. Output is a number on the **Agtron Scale** (0–100):

- **Higher number** = lighter roast = more reflectance
- **Lower number** = darker roast = less reflectance

### Professional Models

| Model | Type | Method | Price |
|-------|------|--------|-------|
| **Agtron M-Basic** | Dedicated coffee spectrophotometer | Ground coffee | ~$8,000 |
| **Agtron E20P** | Entry commercial | Ground + whole bean | ~$4,000 |
| **Tonino (Lighttells)** | Portable colorimeter | Ground coffee | ~$500 |
| **ColorTrack** | Smartphone + clip | Ground coffee | ~$150 |
| **Cropster Roast Eye** | USB colorimeter | Ground coffee | ~$400 |

### SCA Roast Color Tiles

The SCA developed a set of **8 physical tile standards** for visual roast colour comparison (Agtron equivalent 25, 35, 45, 55, 65, 75, 85, 95):

- Used for calibration of electronic Agtron meters
- Available as physical laminated tiles
- Essential for any roastery without an electronic Agtron

### Agtron Measurement Protocol

```
AGTRON MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL

Whole bean:
1. Allow roasted coffee to cool to room temperature (minimum 20 min)
2. Place whole beans in Agtron sample cup
3. Scan; record whole bean Agtron number

Ground measurement:
1. Grind 20g of cooled roasted coffee at medium-fine setting
2. Allow ground to settle (CO₂ release)
3. Fill sample cup; level with spatula (do not compress)
4. Scan; record ground Agtron number

Note: Ground Agtron is typically 20–30 points LOWER than whole bean Agtron
(grinding reveals interior which is lighter than surface)
```

## 3. Moisture Meters — Green Coffee

### Why Moisture Matters

Green coffee must be 10–12% moisture:
- **< 9%** — brittle; uneven roasting; quality loss
- **10–12%** — optimal; stable storage
- **> 13%** — mould risk; unstable; short shelf life

### Moisture Meter Types

| Type | Method | Accuracy | Price |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| **Capacitance meter (Sinar)** | Electrical capacitance through beans | ±0.3% | ~$2,000 |
| **Loss on drying (lab oven)** | Weigh before + after drying at 105°C for 16h | ±0.1% | Oven: ~$500 |
| **Near-infrared (NIR)** | Light absorption | ±0.2% | ~$5,000+ |
| **Portable grain meters (Dickey-John)** | Resistance/capacitance | ±0.5% | ~$300–600 |

### Measurement Protocol (Capacitance Meter)

```
1. Draw representative sample from multiple bag positions
2. Let sample equilibrate to room temperature (30 min minimum)
3. Calibrate meter to coffee setting
4. Insert 100g sample into meter chamber
5. Read moisture %
6. Take 3 readings from different parts of sample; average
7. Log: date, lot, moisture %, sample position
```

## 5. Density Measurement

### Why Density Matters

Bean density correlates with:
- Altitude of growth (higher altitude = denser bean)
- Ripeness at harvest (ripe = denser)
- Roasting heat requirements (denser = needs more energy)
- Expected cup quality (denser usually = more complex)

### Displacement Method

```
BEAN DENSITY MEASUREMENT

Equipment: Graduated cylinder (100mL), scale

1. Zero scale
2. Add 100mL of water to graduated cylinder; mark 100mL line
3. Empty water; dry cylinder
4. Fill cylinder to 100mL mark with whole beans (do not compress)
5. Note volume of beans in cylinder = 100mL (by definition of fill)
6. Weigh filled cylinder; subtract empty cylinder weight
7. Density (g/mL) = bean weight / 100mL

Typical values:
  < 0.65 g/mL = low density (low altitude, commercial)
  0.65–0.75 = medium (standard specialty)
  0.75–0.80 = dense (high altitude specialty)
  > 0.80 = very dense (exceptional — Ethiopian, Kenyan top lots)
```

## 6. Digital Coffee Lab Platforms

### Cropster

**The industry standard for roasting data management:**

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Roast profiling | Real-time temperature logging from any roaster |
| Profile comparison | Overlay current vs reference; identify drift |
| RoR calculation | Automatic rate of rise calculation and display |
| Green inventory | Track green coffee lots, costs, quantities |
| Roast log | Every roast stored with metadata |
| Cupping integration | Log cupping scores against roast profiles |
| Team sharing | Cloud-based; multiple roasters access same data |
| Price | ~$100–400/month depending on plan |

### Artisan (Free Alternative)

Open-source roasting software:
- Full profile logging and comparison
- Works with most Phidgets and Fuji controllers
- Active community; extensive documentation
- Free — no subscription
- Best for: small roasters; hobbyists; budget-conscious

### DiFluid Omni / Umma

New generation of integrated coffee lab tools:
- TDS refractometer + app integration
- Cloud data storage
- Shot-by-shot logging
- Trend analysis over time

## 7. Cupping Lab Setup

### Professional Cupping Lab Requirements (SCA)

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Room temperature** | 20–25°C |
| **Lighting** | Good, neutral (no coloured lights affecting visual) |
| **Odours** | Zero — no perfume, food, or smoke in room |
| **Water** | SCA-standard water (150 mg/L TDS, pH 7.0) |
| **Cupping bowls** | 207–266mL; tempered glass or ceramic |
| **Cupping spoons** | Deep-bowl stainless; 7–10mL capacity |
| **Scale** | 0.1g precision |
| **Kettle** | Temperature-controlled; ±1°C |
| **Grinder** | Clean, dedicated cupping grinder |
| **Rinse cups** | For spoons between cups |
| **Spittoon** | Dark bucket; accessible |
| **Timer** | Accurate to seconds |
| **Score forms** | SCA cupping forms; printed or digital |

## 8. Record-Keeping Systems

### The Lab Data Chain

Every measurement should feed a permanent record:

```
GREEN COFFEE INTAKE
  → Moisture log → Density log → Sample roast log → Cupping score log

PRODUCTION ROASTING
  → Roast profile (Cropster/Artisan) → Agtron log → Roast loss log

ESPRESSO EXTRACTION
  → Dose/yield/time log → TDS/EY log (if refractometer used) → Tasting notes

WATER
  → TDS log → pH log → Alkalinity log → Filter change log

CUPPING
  → SCA score forms → Database (spreadsheet or Cropster)
```
