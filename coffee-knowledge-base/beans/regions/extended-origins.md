## 🇰🇪 Kenya

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production Rank | ~15th globally |
| Annual Production | ~800,000–1,000,000 bags (60kg) |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Altitude Range | 1,400–2,200 masl |
| Harvest | Main: October–December; Fly: June–August |
| Processing | Almost exclusively Washed |
| Grading System | AA, AB, PB (Peaberry), C, E |
| Auction System | Nairobi Coffee Exchange (NCE) |

### Why Kenya Is Unique

Kenya produces some of the **most intense, complex washed coffees on Earth** due to:
- **SL-28 and SL-34 varietals** — bred by Scott Laboratories for drought resistance; produce extraordinary cup
- **Volcanic red clay soils** (Nitisols) with high phosphorus
- **Double washing** process (72-hour fermentation, double wash) → extreme cleanliness
- **Phosphoric acid** naturally present → unique crisp, bright acidity unlike any other origin

### Key Growing Regions

| Region | Altitude (masl) | Cup Notes |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Nyeri** | 1,700–2,200 | Blackcurrant, tomato, intense, full body |
| **Kiambu** | 1,500–1,900 | Complex, citrus, elegant |
| **Murang'a** | 1,400–1,800 | Balanced, fruit, chocolate |
| **Embu** | 1,500–1,900 | Bright, citrus, clean |
| **Meru** | 1,500–2,000 | Bright, fruity |
| **Machakos** | 1,400–1,800 | Lower acidity, mild |

### Varietals

| Varietal | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **SL-28** | Scott Labs selection 1931; drought-tolerant; extraordinary blackcurrant, tomato; Kenya's signature |
| **SL-34** | Scott Labs selection; blackcurrant, full body; slightly less intense than SL-28 |
| **Ruiru 11** | CLR-resistant hybrid; less complex cup; widely planted for disease resistance |
| **Batian** | Newer CLR-resistant; better cup than Ruiru; gaining adoption |

### Kenyan Grading System

| Grade | Screen Size | Character |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| **AA** | Screen 18+ (≥7.22mm) | Largest; premium; most complex |
| **AB** | Screen 15–16 | Common specialty grade; excellent |
| **PB** (Peaberry) | Round single bean | Concentrated; intense |
| **C** | Screen 14 | Lower grade |
| **E** (Elephant) | Double-fused beans | Rare; unusual |

### Cup Profile

```
Kenyan Washed AA:
  Aroma: Blackcurrant, tomato, floral
  Flavour: Blackcurrant jam · Ripe tomato · Orange
  Acidity: Very high · Clean · Phosphoric (crisp, mineral)
  Body: Full · Round · Juicy
  Finish: Very long · Complex · Lingering fruit
  SCA Score: Often 85–92 for top lots
```

## 🇮🇩 Indonesia

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production Rank | 4th globally |
| Annual Production | ~10–12 million bags |
| Primary Species | Robusta (~75%), Arabica (~25%) |
| Altitude Range | Arabica: 1,000–1,800 masl |
| Harvest | June–December (varies by island) |
| Processing | Wet-hulled (Giling Basah) dominant for Arabica; Washed; Natural |

### Wet-Hulled Processing (Giling Basah)

Indonesia's signature processing method — unique to the archipelago:

```
Cherry → Depulp → Short ferment (12–24h) → 
Partial wash → Hull while still at 30–50% moisture → 
Dry to 11–12% moisture
```

**Result:** High moisture during hulling → beans swell and deform → unique earthy, heavy, low-acid character that Indonesian coffees are famous for.

### Key Origins

| Island/Region | Altitude | Primary Process | Cup Notes |
|--------------|---------|----------------|-----------|
| **Sumatra (Mandheling, Lintong, Aceh, Gayo)** | 1,000–1,500 | Wet-hulled | Earthy, cedar, tobacco, dark chocolate, heavy body, low acid |
| **Sulawesi (Toraja, Kalosi)** | 1,400–1,800 | Wet-hulled | Earthy, herbaceous, full body, complex |
| **Flores (Bajawa)** | 1,000–1,800 | Washed/Wet-hulled | Cleaner than Sumatra; earthy, chocolate, citrus |
| **Papua New Guinea** | 1,400–2,000 | Washed | Fruity, earthy, full body; distinct from Indonesian profile |
| **Java** | 900–1,500 | Washed (estate-grown) | Clean, herbal, balanced; lower body than Sumatra |
| **Bali** | 900–1,500 | Wet-hulled | Earthy, mild; often Kintamani designation |

### Cup Profile (Sumatran — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Cedar · Earth · Dark chocolate · Tobacco
Flavour: Deep earthy · Dark chocolate · Herbs · Tobacco
Acidity: Very low · Soft
Body: Very heavy · Syrupy
Finish: Long · Earthy · Lingering
```

## 🇵🇦 Panama

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | Small (~100,000–150,000 bags) but extremely high value |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Altitude Range | 1,200–2,200 masl |
| Key Region | Boquete, Chiriquí Province |
| Famous For | Hacienda La Esmeralda — Gesha/Geisha |

### Why Panama Is Iconic

In **2004**, Hacienda La Esmeralda entered a Gesha varietal (originally from Ethiopia's Gori Gesha forest) into the Best of Panama competition. It scored higher than anything previously seen and sold at auction for a record price — **triggering a global specialty coffee revolution**.

Today Panama Gesha consistently achieves the highest auction prices in the world:
- Record price (2019): **$1,029/lb** (Elida Estate Natural Gesha)
- Typical specialty Gesha: $50–$300/lb

### Cup Profile (Panama Gesha — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Jasmine · Rose · Bergamot · Tropical fruit
Flavour: Passion fruit · Peach · Lychee · Floral honey
Acidity: Delicate · Complex · Citric
Body: Light-medium · Tea-like · Silky
Finish: Extraordinarily long · Floral · Perfumed
SCA Score: Often 90–95+
```

### Key Farms/Estates
| Estate | Notable For |
|--------|------------|
| **Hacienda La Esmeralda** | Pioneered Panama Gesha; multiple record auction prices |
| **Elida Estate** | Record $1,029/lb (2019); natural Gesha |
| **Janson** | Consistent high-quality Gesha |
| **Kotowa** | Bourbon and Caturra alongside Gesha |

## 🇷🇼 Rwanda

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~300,000–400,000 bags |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Primary Varietal | Red Bourbon (dominant) |
| Altitude Range | 1,700–2,200 masl |
| Harvest | March–July |
| Processing | Washed (almost exclusively) |
| Special Challenge | **Potato defect** (bacterial; unique to Great Lakes region) |

### Rwanda's Specialty Journey

Coffee was introduced to Rwanda by German missionaries (~1904). After the 1994 genocide, the country rebuilt its coffee sector as a key economic driver. Today Rwanda is one of Africa's most dynamic specialty origins.

### Cup Profile (Rwandan Bourbon — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Floral · Red fruit · Citrus
Flavour: Pomegranate · Orange · Caramel · Chocolate
Acidity: Bright · Juicy · Citric/malic
Body: Medium · Silky
Finish: Sweet · Fruity · Clean
```

### Potato Defect (Antestia Bug)

Rwanda and Burundi share a unique defect caused by the **Antestia bug** (*Antestiopsis* spp.):
- Bug damages cherry → bacterial infection → specific compound (isovaleric acid, IPMP)
- Results in a cup note described as **raw potato / earthy potato**
- Single affected bean in a batch ruins the entire cup
- **No reliable solution** — hand-sorting is partial mitigation; genetic resistance research ongoing

## 🇸🇻 El Salvador

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~500,000–700,000 bags |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Altitude Range | 900–2,000 masl |
| Harvest | November–March |
| Processing | Washed, Natural, Honey |
| Key Varietals | Bourbon (primary), Pacamara, Pacas |

### Why El Salvador Matters

El Salvador was devastated by decades of civil war and CLR outbreaks but is undergoing a **specialty renaissance**:
- **Bourbon** — some of the world's finest, particularly from Santa Ana volcano region
- **Pacamara** — an El Salvador original (Pacas × Maragogipe); extraordinary complexity when well-grown
- Multiple Cup of Excellence-winning farms

### Key Regions

| Region | Altitude | Cup Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Santa Ana (Apaneca-Ilamatepec)** | 1,000–2,000 | Rich, chocolatey, fruity; volcanic soil |
| **Ahuachapán** | 700–1,500 | Balanced, mild, nutty |
| **Chalatenango** | 1,200–2,100 | Complex, bright, high-grown specialty |

### Cup Profile (Santa Ana Bourbon — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Brown sugar · Caramel · Subtle fruit
Flavour: Chocolate · Caramel · Mild stone fruit · Brown sugar
Acidity: Medium · Soft · Clean
Body: Medium-full · Silky
Finish: Long · Sweet · Chocolate
```
