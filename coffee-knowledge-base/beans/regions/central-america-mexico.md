## 🇭🇳 Honduras

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production Rank | 6th globally (largest in Central America) |
| Annual Production | ~7–8 million bags (60kg) |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Altitude Range | 1,000–1,800 masl |
| Harvest | November–April |
| Processing | Washed (dominant); Natural/Honey growing |
| Regulatory Body | IHCAFE (Instituto Hondureño del Café) |
| Altitude Grades | SHG (Strictly High Grown, >1,200m), HG (High Grown, 900–1,200m) |

> 🌿 Honduras has transformed from a bulk commodity producer to one of Central America's leading specialty origins in just 15 years — driven by IHCAFE support and growing direct-trade relationships.

### Key Regions

| Region | Altitude (masl) | Cup Notes |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Copán** | 1,000–1,500 | Chocolate, caramel, red fruit, balanced |
| **Ocotepeque** | 1,200–1,800 | Citrus, honey, bright acidity |
| **Comayagua** | 1,100–1,500 | Stone fruit, chocolate, medium body |
| **Montecillos** | 1,200–1,700 | Peach, citrus, caramel, complex |
| **Agalta** | 1,000–1,400 | Mild, balanced, nutty |
| **El Paraíso** | 1,000–1,400 | Mild, chocolate, approachable |

### Key Varietals

| Varietal | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Catuai** | Primary varietal; high yield; balanced |
| **Caturra** | Bright, clean; widely planted |
| **Bourbon** | Quality; grown at highest altitudes |
| **Typica** | Traditional; rare; excellent cup |
| **IHCAFE 90** | Local disease-resistant hybrid |
| **Parainema** | CLR-resistant; growing adoption |

### Cup Profile (Montecillos — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Peach · Honey · Floral
Flavour: Ripe peach · Caramel · Citrus zest · Mild chocolate
Acidity: Bright · Clean · Malic
Body: Medium · Smooth
Finish: Sweet · Fruity · Long
```

### Altitude Grading System

| Grade | Altitude | Cup Quality |
|-------|---------|------------|
| **SHG (Strictly High Grown)** | > 1,200 masl | Best; specialty grade |
| **HG (High Grown)** | 900–1,200 masl | Good; commercial specialty |
| **CS (Central Standard)** | 600–900 masl | Commercial; lower quality |

## 🇨🇷 Costa Rica

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~1.5–2 million bags |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica (Robusta banned by law since 1988) |
| Altitude Range | 1,200–1,800 masl |
| Harvest | November–March |
| Processing | Washed, Honey (pioneered here), Natural |
| Regulatory Body | ICAFE (Instituto del Café de Costa Rica) |
| Notable | World's first country to legally ban Robusta cultivation |

### Honey Processing — Costa Rica's Innovation

Costa Rica is widely credited with pioneering the modern **honey processing** movement:

```
Honey Processing Variants:

YELLOW HONEY
  Mucilage removed: 75–80%
  Drying time: ~8 days
  Cup: Clean, sweet, mild fruit

RED HONEY  
  Mucilage removed: 50%
  Drying time: ~12 days
  Cup: Balanced fruit, sweetness, body

BLACK HONEY
  Mucilage removed: 0–10%
  Drying time: ~2–4 weeks (with turning)
  Cup: Intense fruit, heavy body, complex
  Closest to: Natural processing
```

### Micro-Mill Revolution

Costa Rica pioneered the **micro-mill model** — small farms processing their own coffee independently rather than sending to centralised wet mills:

- Allows individual farms to differentiate quality
- Enables honey and natural experimentation
- Higher traceability (single-farm lots vs cooperative blends)
- Significantly higher premiums for top micro-mills

Key micro-mills: La Minita, Café de Altura, Hacienda La Minita, Dota Tarrazu

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|

| Annual Production | ~4–5 million bags |

### Cup Profile (Chiapas Specialty — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Cocoa · Caramel · Light floral
Flavour: Chocolate · Caramel · Mild citrus · Hazelnut
Acidity: Medium-low · Soft · Clean
Body: Medium · Smooth
```

### Mexico's Organic Sector

Mexico has one of the world's largest certified organic coffee sectors:
- ~30% of production is certified organic
- Indigenous community cooperatives (Chiapas, Oaxaca) dominate
- Major exporter of organic certified coffee to US, EU
- Quality varies considerably — certification ≠ specialty grade

## SHB vs HB Altitude Classification

Used across Central America:

| Classification | Altitude | Interpretation |
|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **SHB / SHG** (Strictly Hard Bean / Strictly High Grown) | > 1,200–1,400 masl (varies by country) | Best quality; most complex; hardest bean |
| **HB / HG** (Hard Bean / High Grown) | 900–1,200 masl | Good quality; commercial specialty |
| **Semi-Hard Bean** | 600–900 masl | Commercial; lower complexity |
| **Soft Bean** | < 600 masl | Low quality; mass market |

> 🔬 *Higher altitude = cooler temperatures = slower cherry maturation = more complex sugars and acids develop = harder, denser bean = better cup quality.*
