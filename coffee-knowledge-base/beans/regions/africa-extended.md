## Overview: The Great Lakes Coffee Belt

The Great Lakes region of East/Central Africa — spanning Rwanda, Burundi, eastern DRC, and parts of Uganda — sits on ancient volcanic soils at 1,400–2,200 masl with a unique climate: two wet seasons creating consistent bi-annual flowering and harvest in some areas.

These origins share:
- **Red Bourbon as dominant varietal** (from Belgian missionary introduction ~1900)
- **Washed processing** (almost exclusively)
- **Station/washing station model** (smallholder farmers bring cherry to communal wet mills)
- **Extremely high cup quality** in ideal conditions
- **Potato defect risk** (unique to this region — *Antestia* bug damage)

## 🇷🇼 Rwanda

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~300,000–400,000 bags (60kg) |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Primary Varietal | Red Bourbon |
| Altitude Range | 1,600–2,200 masl |
| Harvest | March–July |
| Processing | Washed (almost exclusively) |

### Rwanda's Coffee Story

Coffee arrived in Rwanda with Belgian missionaries (~1904). After the 1994 genocide, coffee was identified as a key economic recovery driver. Today, the **Rwandan government** directly invests in washing station infrastructure, Q Grader training, and export promotion. The transformation from commodity to specialty is one of the most remarkable in modern coffee history.

### Key Regions

| Region | Altitude (masl) | Cup Notes |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Nyamasheke** | 1,700–2,100 | Floral, citrus, sweet, elegant |
| **Karongi (Kibuye)** | 1,700–2,100 | Orange blossom, caramel, tropical |
| **Rulindo** | 1,700–2,000 | Red fruit, citrus, caramel |
| **Nyanza** | 1,500–1,900 | Balanced, mild fruit, clean |
| **Huye (Butare)** | 1,700–2,100 | Citrus, complex, floral |

### Cup Profile (Rwanda Bourbon — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Pomegranate · Floral · Citrus · Caramel
Flavour: Orange · Red fruit · Brown sugar · Mild chocolate
Acidity: Bright · Juicy · Malic/citric
Body: Medium · Silky
Finish: Sweet · Fruity · Clean
SCA Score: Often 85–90+
```

### Washing Station Model

Rwanda operates a **station model** — smallholder farmers (often with < 0.5 hectares) bring cherry to communal washing stations (sometimes called "stations" or "cooperatives"):

- 1 washing station typically serves 400–2,000 farmers
- Station controls fermentation, washing, drying quality
- Lot identity preserved at station level
- Enables specialty purchasing by washing station name

**Famous stations:** Musasa, Nyamasheke, Sholi, Gitesi, Kageyo, Gakenke

## 🇧🇮 Burundi

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~200,000–300,000 bags |
| Primary Species | 100% Arabica |
| Primary Varietal | Red Bourbon |
| Altitude Range | 1,500–2,000 masl |
| Harvest | April–July |
| Processing | Washed (primarily) |
| Regulatory Body | ARFIC (Autorité de Régulation de la Filière Café) |

### Why Burundi Matters

Burundi shares Rwanda's high-altitude Bourbon character and has some of the world's most exciting specialty coffees. However, political instability and infrastructure challenges have historically limited its specialty potential. The past decade has seen significant improvement.

### Key Regions

| Region | Altitude | Cup Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Kayanza** | 1,700–2,100 | Peach, jasmine, honey, complex |
| **Ngozi** | 1,600–2,000 | Citrus, floral, caramel |
| **Kirundo** | 1,500–1,800 | Balanced, mild fruit |
| **Muyinga** | 1,500–1,800 | Mild, approachable |
| **Bujumbura (area)** | 1,400–1,700 | Commercial grade |

### Cup Profile (Kayanza — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Peach · Jasmine · Honey
Flavour: Stone fruit · Caramel · Citrus · Floral honey
Acidity: Bright · Clean
Body: Medium · Round
Finish: Long · Sweet
```

### Potato Defect (See also: Rwanda section)

Both Rwanda and Burundi have the **potato defect** — caused by *Antestia* bug damage to the cherry, introducing *isopropyl methoxypyrazine* (IPMP) — a compound with an intensely raw potato smell.

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Odour threshold | Extremely low (~0.002 μg/L) — detectable at vanishing concentrations |
| Impact | One affected bean ruins an entire cup |
| Frequency | Unpredictable; varies by season, altitude, pest management |
| Detection | Cannot be detected in green coffee — only after roasting |
| Solutions | Better pest management; optical sorting; consumer acceptance |
| Varietal resistance | None known; research ongoing |

## 🇺🇬 Uganda

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production Rank | 8th–9th globally |
| Annual Production | ~5–6 million bags |
| Primary Species | **Robusta (~80%), Arabica (~20%)** |
| Altitude (Arabica) | 1,200–2,200 masl |
| Harvest (Arabica) | October–February (Mt Elgon, Mt. Rwenzori) |
| Processing | Washed (Arabica), natural Robusta |

### Uganda's Dual Coffee Identity

Uganda is the **birthplace of Coffea canephora (Robusta)** — wild Robusta trees grow naturally in Uganda's forests. Simultaneously, it produces some of Africa's finest specialty Arabica at high altitude.

### Arabica Regions

| Region | Mountain | Altitude | Cup Notes |
|--------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Mt Elgon (Sipi Falls area)** | Mt Elgon | 1,600–2,200 | Citrus, stone fruit, floral, bright |
| **Mt Rwenzori** | Rwenzori | 1,500–2,200 | Complex, fruity, clean |
| **Bugisu** | Mt Elgon | 1,400–2,000 | Balanced, fruit, chocolate |

### Fine Robusta / Specialty Robusta

Uganda produces some of the world's finest specialty-grade Robusta:

| Region | Altitude | Cup Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Kalangala (islands)** | 900–1,200 | Mild, clean, chocolate |
| **Mt Elgon foothills** | 1,000–1,400 | Earthy, mild |

**SCA-adapted Robusta cupping score:** Fine Ugandan Robusta can score 80+ on modified Robusta evaluation scale (being developed by SCA/CQI).

### Arabica Cup Profile (Mt Elgon — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Floral · Citrus · Mild fruit
Flavour: Stone fruit · Caramel · Mild chocolate · Clean
Acidity: Bright · Clean
Body: Medium
Finish: Sweet · Moderate length
```

### Country Overview

| Parameter | Data |
|-----------|------|
| Production | ~800,000–1,000,000 bags |
| Primary Species | Arabica (~75%), Robusta (~25%) |
| Altitude (Arabica) | 1,400–2,200 masl |
| Harvest | June–October (Kilimanjaro); October–February (Southern Highlands) |
| Processing | Washed (primary) |
| Regulatory Body | Tanzania Coffee Board (TCB) |

### Key Regions

| Region | Landmark | Altitude | Cup Notes |
|--------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Kilimanjaro (Moshi)** | Mt Kilimanjaro | 1,400–2,000 | Bright citrus, chocolate, full body |
| **Arusha** | Mt Meru | 1,400–1,800 | Fruity, balanced, chocolate |
| **Southern Highlands (Mbeya, Mbinga)** | Southern Highlands | 1,600–2,200 | Complex, citrus, floral |
| **Kagera (Bukoba)** | Lake Victoria | 1,200–1,600 | Mild, earthy, commercial |

### Key Varietals

**Bourbon, Typica (Kent), N39 (Tanzania-developed hybrid), Arusha (local Typica), Peaberry**

### Tanzanian Peaberry (AA/PB)

Tanzania is particularly famous for its **Peaberry** grade:
- Peaberry = single round bean forms instead of two flat beans (10–15% of any crop)
- Smaller size → more concentrated flavour per bean
- More even roasting (round shape)
- Commands significant premium: often 20–50% above flat-bean equivalent

### Cup Profile (Kilimanjaro — benchmark)
```
Aroma: Citrus · Dark chocolate · Mild fruit
Flavour: Orange · Dark chocolate · Caramel · Full body
Acidity: Bright · Clean · Malic
Body: Full · Round
Finish: Long · Chocolatey · Clean
```

## 🌍 Emerging African Origins

### Congo (DRC)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Altitude | 1,400–2,100 masl (Kivu region) |
| Potential | Extraordinary terroir; high-altitude Bourbon |
| Challenges | Infrastructure, conflict, logistics |
| Cup notes | Floral, citrus, stone fruit — similar to Rwanda/Burundi at best |
| Status | Small but growing specialty exports |

### Cameroon

| Parameter | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Species | Both Arabica (Western Highlands) and Robusta |
| Altitude (Arabica) | 1,000–1,800 masl |
| Key region | Bamenda Highlands (NW Province) |
| Cup notes | Mild, chocolate, earthy, balanced |
| Status | Mostly commodity; some specialty development |

### Zambia / Zimbabwe / Malawi

| Country | Key Detail | Cup Notes |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Zambia** | Terrific highland potential; very small production | Citrus, clean, bright — similar to Kenya |
| **Zimbabwe** | Historical Arabica; collapsed post-2000; slowly recovering | Tobacco, fruit, heavy body |
| **Malawi** | Shire Highlands; growing specialty | Floral, mild, clean |
