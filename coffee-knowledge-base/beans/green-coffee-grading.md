## What Is Green Coffee?

Green coffee is the **raw, unroasted seed** of the coffee cherry — stripped of its fruit flesh (pulp) and parchment, dried to appropriate moisture levels, and prepared for export.

Understanding green coffee quality is essential for:
- Roasters sourcing quality raw material
- Q Graders evaluating lots
- Importers and traders assessing value
- Producers optimising their processing

---

## Physical Assessment Framework

### 1. Moisture Content

| Moisture % | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| < 9% | Too dry — brittle, risk of fracturing during roasting |
| **10–12%** | ✅ Optimal — stable, good shelf life |
| 12–13% | Acceptable — slightly elevated; monitor storage |
| > 13% | Too wet — mould risk; unstable; will deteriorate |

**Measurement tool:** Digital grain moisture meter (e.g., Sinar, Dickey-John)

**Standard:** SCA requires 10–12% moisture for green coffee assessment. Coffee must be reconditioned if outside range before cupping.

---

### 2. Water Activity (Aw)

Water activity measures **available moisture** (not total moisture) — the portion that supports microbial growth:

| Water Activity (Aw) | Risk |
|---------------------|------|
| > 0.80 | High mould risk |
| 0.70–0.80 | Moderate risk |
| **< 0.70** | ✅ Safe — no mould growth |
| < 0.60 | Very stable |

Water activity is more important than moisture % for food safety determination.

---

### 3. Bean Density

Higher-altitude, slower-maturing beans = denser:

| Density (g/mL) | Typical Source |
|---------------|---------------|
| 0.60–0.65 | Low altitude, fast-growing |
| **0.65–0.75** | ✅ Standard specialty |
| 0.75–0.80 | High altitude, dense |
| > 0.80 | Exceptional density (e.g., Kenyan, Ethiopian) |

**Why density matters for roasting:**
- Denser beans require more heat energy to roast (lower thermal conductivity)
- Higher charge temperatures needed
- More development time required
- Produces more complex, sweeter cup when properly roasted

**Measurement:** Displacement method (fill container, weigh, calculate g/mL)

---

### 4. Screen Size (Sieve Grading)

Screen size measured in 1/64 inch increments:

| Screen # | Size (mm) | Used In |
|---------|----------|---------|
| 8 | 3.18 | Small bean (some peaberry) |
| 10 | 3.97 | Small |
| 12 | 4.76 | Below average |
| 13 | 5.16 | Standard |
| 14 | 5.56 | Exchange grade minimum |
| 15 | 5.95 | AB grade (Kenya) |
| 16 | 6.35 | Standard specialty |
| 17 | 6.75 | Large specialty |
| 18 | 7.14 | AA (Kenya); premium |
| 19 | 7.54 | Very large |
| 20 | 7.94 | Exceptional |

**Importance:** Screen size indicates bean development consistency. Mixed sizes = uneven roasting (small beans over-roast before large ones are done).

---

### 5. Colour

| Colour | Interpretation |
|--------|--------------|
| Blue-green / grey-green | Fresh, well-stored, high quality |
| Bright green | Recent crop, fresh |
| Pale green / yellowish | Aged; beginning to fade |
| Yellow / brown | Old crop; oxidised; significant quality loss |
| Black spots | Mould or fungal damage |

## SCA Green Coffee Defect Classification

### Primary Defects (most severe — any presence in Grade 1 = disqualification)

| Defect | Description | Cup Impact |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Full black bean** | Entire bean black — advanced decomposition | Severe: phenolic, rotten |
| **Full sour bean** | Brown/fermented bean — acidic odour | Severe: vinegar, fermented |
| **Dried cherry/pod** | Whole dried fruit remaining | Severe: extreme ferment |
| **Fungus-damaged** | Visible mould growth | Severe: musty, mouldy |
| **Foreign material** | Rocks, sticks, metal | Physical hazard |
| **Severe insect damage** | >1/3 of bean damaged | Severe: sour, fermented |

### Secondary Defects (max 5 for SCA Grade 1)

| Defect | Description | Cup Impact |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Partial black** | Part of bean black | Mild: slightly phenolic |
| **Partial sour** | Part of bean brown/fermented | Mild: slight sourness |
| **Floater** | Porous, light bean (floats in water) | Bland, hollow |
| **Immature/unripe** | Pale; silverskin still adhering | Grassy, astringent |
| **Withered** | Thin, wrinkled — drought or disease | Woody, thin |
| **Shell (elephant ear)** | Genetic deformity; hollow centre | Uneven roasting |
| **Broken/chipped/cut** | Physical damage during processing | Fast extraction; burnt |
| **Slight insect damage** | Small holes, minor damage | Minor quality impact |
| **Hull/husk** | Dried cherry skin remaining | Woody |

### Defect Equivalents for Grading

Not all defects count equally. The SCA uses equivalents:

| Primary Defect | Equivalent Count |
|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 full black bean | = 1 primary defect |
| 1 full sour bean | = 1 primary defect |
| 1 dried cherry | = 1 primary defect |
| 1 piece foreign material | = 1 primary defect |
| 2–5 fungus-damaged | = 1 primary defect |

| Secondary Defect | Equivalents (per 1 defect count) |
|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| 3 partial black | = 1 |
| 3 partial sour | = 1 |
| 5 floaters | = 1 |
| 5 immature | = 1 |
| 5 withered | = 1 |
| 5 shells | = 1 |
| 5 broken/chipped | = 1 |
| 10 slight insect | = 1 |

## SCA Green Coffee Classification System

### Grading Table

| SCA Grade | Primary Defects (per 350g) | Secondary Defects | Cup Score |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Specialty (Grade 1)** | 0 | Max 5 | ≥ 80 |
| **Premium (Grade 2)** | 0 | Max 8 | 78–79 |
| **Exchange (Grade 3)** | Max 3 | Max 23 | — |
| **Below Standard (Grade 4)** | 4–11 | Max 24 | — |
| **Off Grade (Grade 5)** | >11 | >24 | — |

**Additional Grade 1 requirements:**
- Zero quakers (unripe beans that don't brown during roasting)
- Moisture: 10–12%
- No unacceptable cup defects (phenol, Rio, ferment, mould)

## Quaker Detection

**Quakers** = unripe green beans that fail to brown properly during roasting, remaining pale/tan while surrounding beans darken:

```
Visual after roasting:
Normal beans: ████████████ (brown)
Quakers:      ░░░░░░░░░░░░ (pale, blonde)
```

**In cup:** Peanut-like, bland, papery — ruins cup quality even in small quantities.

**Origin:** Unripe cherry at harvest. Solutions: better selective picking (hand-pick only red cherries), flotation (unripe beans float in water), optical sorting at mill.

**Grade 1 requires:** Zero quakers in 100g sample.

## Physical Assessment Protocol (Green Grading)

### Step-by-Step

```
1. SAMPLE PREPARATION
   □ Weigh out 350g representative sample
   □ Spread on white grading tray
   □ Note: colour, odour, general appearance

2. DEFECT ANALYSIS
   □ Sort through entire 350g by hand
   □ Remove and categorise each defect
   □ Count primary defects
   □ Count secondary defects (using equivalents)
   □ Record on grading form

3. SCREEN SIZE
   □ Pass sample through nested sieves
   □ Weigh retained at each screen
   □ Calculate % at each size
   □ Determine dominant screen and uniformity

4. MOISTURE
   □ 100g subsample to moisture meter
   □ Record moisture %
   □ Record water activity if meter available

5. DENSITY
   □ Displacement measurement
   □ Record g/mL

6. COLOUR
   □ Visual assessment
   □ Agtron meter if available

7. SENSORY (after roasting and resting)
   □ Sample roast to medium-light
   □ Rest 8–24 hours
   □ Cup to SCA protocol
   □ Score against SCA form
```

## Green Coffee Storage Science

### Storage Conditions

| Parameter | Optimal | Risk if Wrong |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Temperature | 15–25°C | High temp → faster aging; accelerates chemical reactions |
| Relative Humidity | 50–70% | High RH → moisture absorption → mould; Low RH → over-drying |
| Ventilation | Good airflow | Poor ventilation → CO₂/moisture buildup |
| Light | Dark | Light exposure → oxidation of oils |
| Off-odours | None nearby | Coffee absorbs odours readily |

### Packaging Types

| Packaging | Description | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **GrainPro bag** (inside jute) | Modified atmosphere; blocks moisture | Specialty coffee; long transit |
| **Jute/burlap sack** | Traditional; breathable | Short-term, temperate climates |
| **Vacuum-sealed foil** | Airtight; maximum freshness | Competition lots; premium samples |
| **Big bags (1 tonne)** | Bulk commercial | High-volume commodity |

### Aging & Freshness

| Coffee Age | Character |
|-----------|-----------|
| Crop year (< 1 year) | Fresh; bright; best quality |
| Past crop (1–2 years) | Fading; losing brightness; woody notes |
| Old crop (2–3 years) | Significant quality degradation; baggy, woody |
| Aged/vintage (intentional) | Some Indonesian coffees intentionally aged (earthy development) |

**Crop years:** Coffee is designated by harvest year. "New crop" appears at origin approximately November–May depending on hemisphere.

## Sourcing Green Coffee

### Supply Chain Options for Roasters

| Source | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Specialty importer** | Curated lots; sensory pre-screened; samples available | Specialty roasters; quality focus |
| **Green trader/broker** | Large volumes; price negotiation | Commercial roasters |
| **Direct from exporter** | Skip importer layer; build direct relationship | Established specialty roasters |
| **Direct from farm/coop** | Maximum transparency; relationship coffee | Small-batch specialty; direct trade |
| **Spot market** | Available immediately; no forward contract | Emergency; fill-ins |

### Evaluation Before Buying

1. **Request sample** (typically 100–200g roasted or 200–400g green)
2. **Sample roast** to medium-light development
3. **Rest 24–48 hours**
4. **Cup against SCA protocol**
5. **Score and evaluate**: meets quality threshold?
6. **Assess price vs quality** relationship
7. **Check provenance**: farm, altitude, varietal, process, harvest year documented?
8. **Place order**: agree price, weight, delivery, payment terms
